Why Inconel wrecks unprepared shops

Nickel-based superalloys work-harden aggressively, cook tooling if the strategy is wrong, and hold the heat that a shop needs to dump into the chips. Job shops that only touch it occasionally learn that on your part. We don't.

How we run Inconel 718 and 625

Sharp, correctly-geometried carbide with the right coating. Aggressive feed to keep the cutting edge below the work-hardened layer. Copious high-pressure coolant. Programmed toolpaths that keep engagement steady. Regular inserts, not 'let's see what happens.'

What we make in Inconel

Frac pump plungers and valve internals, wellhead sealing components, downhole tool bodies, aerospace bracketry and structural components, defense actuator parts. If it's a nickel-based superalloy and it has to be right, we've probably made one.

Reasonable expectations on schedule and price

Inconel is not fast material and it is not cheap material. What we commit to, we deliver. If your last shop went two weeks late and blamed the alloy — that's them, not the material.
