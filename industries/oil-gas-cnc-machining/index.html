Materials We Machine (And Why It Matters) 

In oil and gas, material selection determines whether your part lasts six months or six years. We machine exotic alloys, corrosion-resistant grades, and high-strength steels that survive extreme pressure, sour gas, torque, heat, erosion, and corrosive environments.

Exotic Alloys for Extreme Conditions

Inconel 718 & 625
Ideal for high-heat, high-pressure, and corrosive environments. Perfect for downhole tools, high-temperature valve trim, and subsea hardware. We specialize in Inconel machining—correct speeds, feeds, and tooling to avoid work-hardening.

Hastelloy C-276
When H₂S and CO₂ threaten to destroy everything, Hastelloy C-276 is the answer. Used for sour service components and chemical injection parts.

Monel 400 & K-500
Corrosion-resistant nickel-copper alloy used in offshore and subsea systems. Excellent for saltwater and high-cycle fatigue environments.

Duplex & Super Duplex Stainless

High-strength, corrosion-resistant alloys for pressure-rated components, subsea housings, and offshore tooling. Twice the strength of 316 stainless with superior pitting resistance.

High-Strength Alloy Steels (The Oilfield Workhorses)

4140 & 4340 Alloy Steel
Heat-treatable, extremely strong, and ideal for drill collars, downhole assemblies, and heavy-duty shafts.

4130 Chromium-Moly
Strong, tough, and weldable—used for tubular components and structural oilfield parts.

Stainless Steels for Pressure & Corrosion Resistance

316 / 316L Stainless
The standard for moderate corrosion resistance in oilfield hardware and valve components.

17-4 PH Stainless
Precipitation-hardened stainless steel used for high-strength parts such as valve stems, shafts, and precision fasteners.

13Cr (F6NM)
NACE-compliant stainless steel designed specifically for sour gas environments.
