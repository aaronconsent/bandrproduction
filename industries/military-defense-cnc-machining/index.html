Defense-Grade Materials We Machine

In military and defense manufacturing, material selection determines whether a component survives recoil, blast pressure, heat, corrosion, or extreme mechanical stress. B&R Productions machines the alloys trusted across weapons systems, ground vehicles, UAV platforms, and tactical equipment—materials chosen for strength, stability, and reliability when failure is not an option.

We understand how each alloy behaves under heat, tool pressure, chip load, and vibration. That knowledge is the difference between parts that hold tolerance under fire—and parts that don’t.

High-Temperature Superalloys for Demanding Defense Environments

Inconel 718 & 625
These nickel superalloys maintain strength at extreme temperatures and under repeated stress, making them ideal for missile components, exhaust housings, thermal shields, and propulsion interfaces. We machine Inconel with controlled toolpaths, feeds, and coolant strategies to prevent work hardening and to keep critical features within spec.

Hastelloy C-276
Hastelloy offers exceptional corrosion resistance in chemically aggressive or high-heat defense environments. Common in sensor housings, chemical-delivery systems, and components exposed to corrosive battlefield conditions or extreme thermal cycling.

Monel 400 & K-500
Monel combines corrosion resistance with high fatigue strength—well suited for naval defense applications, components exposed to moisture or salt spray, and vibration-heavy environments. Frequently used for fasteners, brackets, mounts, and ruggedized hardware on marine and coastal platforms.

 
Defense-Grade Aluminum for Lightweight Strength

2024-T3 / T4
Excellent strength-to-weight ratio and fatigue resistance. Ideal for structural brackets, UAV components, and lightweight assemblies in ground and air systems.

6061-T6
Highly machinable, stable, and versatile. Common in housings, mounts, weapons accessories, and general-purpose defense components where reliability matters more than exotic material properties.

7075-T6 / T651
One of the strongest aluminum alloys available. Perfect for high-load components such as weapon mounts, vehicle brackets, and force-bearing fittings that need maximum strength without extra weight.

 
Titanium Alloys for High Strength-to-Weight Defense Hardware

Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5)
The backbone titanium alloy of modern defense systems. Strong, corrosion-resistant, and capable of handling heat and impact loads. Used in weapon housings, heavy-duty mounts, UAV structures, and aircraft hardware.

Ti-6Al-2Sn-4Zr-2Mo
Designed for elevated-temperature service. Ideal for missile housings, propulsion parts, and high-heat components where thermal expansion and dimensional stability must stay under control.

 
Stainless Steels for Rugged, Field-Ready Components

17-4 PH Stainless
High strength and excellent toughness. Common in actuator shafts, fire-control components, rugged linkages, and hardened brackets used across military systems.

15-5 PH Stainless
Offers strength with added corrosion resistance. Used for precision housings, structural parts, and components that must handle repeated impacts or environmental exposure.

316 / 316L Stainless
Corrosion-resistant and stable in moisture, fuel, salt, and chemical environments. Ideal for naval equipment, field electronics, and vehicle-mounted assemblies.

 
High-Strength Alloy Steels for Heavy-Duty Military Components

4140 & 4340 Alloy Steel
Heat-treatable steels used for gun components, high-load shafts, vehicle linkages, and structural elements that must survive extreme mechanical forces and impact cycles.

300M / 4340M
Ultra-high-strength steel commonly used in landing gear, fasteners, actuators, and high-stress components on military aircraft and heavy tactical platforms.

Why Material Expertise Matters

Every military alloy brings its own machining challenges—heat buildup, tool wear, chip evacuation issues, dimensional shift, or fatigue sensitivity. B&R Productions selects the right tooling, cutting strategy, fixturing, and coolant system for each material to ensure your components:

  • Hold tolerance under stress
  • Maintain structural integrity
  • Survive recoil, vibration, heat, and field conditions
  • Fit and function flawlessly in the final assembly

In defense applications, material expertise isn’t optional—it’s the difference between a component that holds up in the field and one that compromises the mission.
