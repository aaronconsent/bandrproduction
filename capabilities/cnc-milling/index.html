CNC Milling Services FAQ

Find answers to common questions about our CNC milling services in Texas. Learn how B&R Productions machines prototypes, tight-tolerance components, and full production runs using vertical and horizontal mills for oil & gas, aerospace, defense, and industrial manufacturing.

What is CNC milling used for?

CNC milling is used to machine complex parts with precision, including valve bodies, brackets, housings, and tooling plates. It’s ideal for prototypes, low-volume runs, and high-volume production.

Which materials can you machine with CNC milling?

We mill a wide range of materials: stainless steels (17-4PH, 15-5PH), alloy steels (4140, 4340), aluminum (6061, 7075), and exotic alloys such as Inconel, Hastelloy, and Monel.

Do you provide prototype CNC milling?

Yes. We specialize in prototype CNC milling services, offering rapid turnaround for design validation, functional testing, and pre-production parts.

What industries rely on your CNC milling services?

We support multiple sectors, including oil & gas, aerospace, defense, heavy equipment, and general manufacturing, with proven experience machining critical parts.

What tolerances can you hold on CNC-milled parts?

We regularly achieve tolerances down to ±0.0005” depending on geometry, material, and fixturing requirements.

Do you offer inspection and quality reporting?

Yes. All CNC-milled components undergo CMM inspection and full traceability reporting, with processes certified to ISO 9001:2015.

What’s the lead time for CNC milling services?

Lead times vary by complexity and volume, but we offer fast turnaround for prototypes and competitive delivery schedules for production runs.
