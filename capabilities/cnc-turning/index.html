CNC Turning Services FAQ

Explore common questions about our CNC turning services in Texas. Learn how B&R Production machines bushings, collars, and long-shaft components with tight tolerances for oil & gas, aerospace, defense, and industrial applications using advanced CNC lathes and in-house inspection processes.

What CNC turning services do you offer?

We provide production and prototype CNC turning, long-part machining, deep bore turning, precision threading, and tight-tolerance finishing for oil & gas, aerospace, defense, and industrial applications.

What tolerances can you hold on turned parts?

Typical work holds ±0.001 in; critical features can be held to ±0.0005 in where design, fixturing, and process control allow. All tolerances are validated against the print and applicable standards.

Which materials do you machine for CNC turning?

We work with carbon and alloy steels such as 4140 and 4340, stainless steels including 304, 316, and 17-4PH, nickel-based alloys like Inconel, Monel, and Hastelloy, along with aluminum, bronze, and other engineer-grade metals specified on your drawing.

What tolerances can you hold with CNC turning?

We regularly hold turning tolerances down to ±0.0005”, depending on material, geometry, and inspection requirements.

Do you support oil & gas standards such as API 6A?

Yes. We regularly manufacture API-relevant components to print, including wellhead and downhole parts, with inspection documentation aligned to customer and industry requirements.

What quality system do you use for inspection and traceability?

Our processes follow  practices with documented in-process and final inspection. CMM and calibrated gauges are used for critical features. Material certs, heat-lot traceability, and FAIR/PPAP are available upon request.

What file types do you accept for quotes and manufacturing?

STEP, IGES, and native 3D models are preferred. We also accept PDF and DXF prints. Please include material, quantity, required tolerances, finishes, and any special notes for the most accurate quote.

What are typical lead times and do you offer rush delivery?

Lead times depend on complexity, material availability, special processes, and lot size. Expedited options are available—submit your drawing and timing requirements for a confirmed schedule.

Can you provide finishing and secondary processes?

Yes. We coordinate heat treatment, coatings, and finishing per print such as nitriding, zinc phosphate, and passivation. Surface finish targets down to Ra 32 µin or better can be achieved when specified.

How do I request a quote for CNC turning?

Send your drawing or model with quantities, material, tolerances, and any certifications required. Our team will perform a technical review and respond with pricing, lead time, and a manufacturing plan.
