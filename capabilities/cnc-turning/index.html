Frequently asked questions

What's your maximum turning envelope?

Samsung SL-65A handles the largest work — up to about 25" swing over the bed and 80" between centers for heavy-roughing oilfield parts. Our Hi-Eco pair runs at the small end (12" swing, 21" between centers). Full envelope coverage across the 7-machine mix.

What tolerances do you routinely hold on CNC turning?

±0.0005" on critical dimensions is routine. Tighter with a specific setup and fresh inserts. Every critical feature gets first-article CMM verification and in-process gauging.

Do you turn Inconel and Super Duplex?

Yes — weekly. We keep coated-carbide inserts (AlTiN, TiCN-TiN) matched to each alloy on the shelf, high-pressure through-tool coolant on the Hi-Tech and Mega, and disciplined feeds so we don't work-harden the material. Send a print, get real quotes.

Can you handle long-bore drilling on turned parts?

For L/D under about 10, yes — we drill on the lathe with coated carbide and peck cycles. For deeper (L/D 15+ oilfield tool bodies, hydraulic cylinders), we use our UNISIG-class deep-hole drilling machine for sub-thousandth-per-foot straightness.

What's your typical lead time on turned parts?

1–3 days for stocked-material prototypes (existing customer). 1–3 weeks for small production runs. Longer if the alloy needs to come from the mill. Rig-down emergency work: same-day to next-day when material is on the shelf.

What material traceability do you provide?

Full heat/lot documentation on every job. Mill Test Reports (MTRs), certificates of conformance, first-article inspection reports, and customer-specific ITPs on request. Standard for oilfield, aerospace, and defense work.
